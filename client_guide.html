The Philosophy
Perspective

Why your photographs matter.

Your wedding day will move faster than you think. Months of planning become hours. Hours become memories. Before long you'll find yourself wishing you could revisit moments you barely had time to feel the first time around.

That's what your photographs are for.

They're the one thing from the day that actually grows in value. The flowers wilt and the playlist ends, but the images only get more precious as the years stack up.

The Story Behind The Name

The name comes from the Lyra constellation. In Greek mythology, it represents the lyre of Orpheus, a small harp said to make music beautiful enough to tame wild beasts and soothe any sorrow.

For me, though, the inspiration is centered on its five brightest stars. I have given each one a meaning of my own—the five brand pillars behind everything I do:

  • ★ Connection

    The best photographs happen when people feel comfortable being themselves.

  • ★ Artistry

    Anyone can take a picture. Timeless takes intention.

Behind The Lens

Meet Dakota

I've been creating photographs since 2012, with over a decade spent behind the camera. On top of my own work, I shoot 25 or more weddings a year for other studios and companies, so a wedding day is well-worn territory for me. Before launching Haus of Lyra, I spent years in business development, marketing, and creative work, handling branding, commercial shoots, and full campaigns—all under real deadlines where there are no second takes.

Here's what that means for your wedding: I know how to read a room, find the best light fast, keep a timeline on track, and capture the moments that disappear in a blink, all while staying calm and out of the way. You won't have to manage me. That's the job.

Off the clock, you'll find me catering to the excessive demands of my two cats, Smokey and Storm, or hanging out with my niece and nephew.

The Purpose
My Vision

Why I do this.

A wedding day is made of so much more than the big moments.

It's the details. The quirks. The traditions only your family understands. The inside joke that takes down an entire table. The thing your grandmother always says.

Some of my favorite images look completely ordinary to a stranger—a folded handkerchief, a particular song, a glance held a beat too long across a crowded room. But to you, those frames carry an entire story, a meaning and a symbolism that speaks volumes.

That's what I'm really there for. Not just the first kiss and the cake, but the small, loaded, easy-to-miss moments you'll want to feel again and again.

More Than a Photographer

The person you hire isn't just making images. Aside from your partner, they're the one person with you for the entire day, from the quiet nerves in the morning to the last song of the night. That's a lot of trust to hand someone.

You want that person genuinely in your corner, not just there for a paycheck. I take that to heart. I care about the couples I work with, and I stay connected with them long after the wedding is over.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you offer video too?

I do, wedding film is something I love. The one thing I can't do is shoot photo and video at the same wedding (I'm one person, and both deserve my full attention). So if you want both, I'll happily cover one and point you to a trusted partner for the other.

Do you travel?

Absolutely. I'm based in Des Moines, Iowa, and I love a good destination wedding. Send me your location and I'll take care of the details.

Do you carry backup equipment?

Always. Multiple cameras, lenses, storage, and backups, so your day is never riding on a single piece of gear.
